# 🐈 Cat Tail Language Dataset

## 📖 Introduction
This project provides a comprehensive mapping of 10 common cat tail behaviors. By translating feline body language (physical actions + emotional meaning) into human-understandable terms, this dataset serves as a foundational reference for pet-related application development, behavioral recognition model labeling, and asset management. 

To understand a cat's mood, think of their tail as a human arm. All file names follow the PascalCase naming convention to ensure programmatic safety and easy integration.

## 📝 Detailed Behavior Descriptions

* **1. Tail high up (like an antenna)**
  Happy greeting ("Hi, how are you!").
* **2. Tail up with a hook at the tip**
  Curious and friendly, inviting play just like a human waving someone over.
* **3. Tail up and quivering (phantom spraying)**
  Extremely excited and overjoyed ("Oh yeah, I'm so happy!").
* **4. Sweeping or heavily thumping the ground**
  Impatient, angry, or annoyed. A clear warning to stay away (similar to a human angrily slamming a table).
* **5. Puffed-up tail**
  On high alert and ready to attack (similar to a human winding up for a physical fight).
* **6. Tail tucked between legs**
  Scared, anxious, or submissive.
* **7. Tip flicking or softly tapping (when called)**
  Acknowledging you but being dismissive ("Yeah, I hear you, stop bothering me").
* **8. Sitting formally with the tail wrapped around the body**
  Completely relaxed with nothing to do (similar to a human comfortably tucking their hands into their pockets).
* **9. Tail held level while walking**
  Patrolling the area, just like a human taking a leisurely stroll and looking around.
* **10. Tail entwined around you**
  Showing deep affection and seeking cuddles (similar to a human giving an open-armed hug). Give them some love!
